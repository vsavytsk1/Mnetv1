A Möbius strip lives inside a dodecahedron of open questions.

What this is

A single-file interactive visualization. A Möbius strip (one half-twist, one boundary, Euler characteristic χ = 0) sits inside a dodecahedron whose twelve faces each carry an open question in modern physics. You can place up to twelve pentagons on the strip — twelve, because a dodecahedron has twelve faces. Hold-pause on one and the local dodecahedral neighborhood emerges from the strip's tangent plane, folding both above and below.

The Möbius is the calculation. The dodecahedron is what the calculation is about. The gap between them — between what the math says LOCALLY (the amber tangent-plane flower) and what the math admits GLOBALLY (the lemon-gold rings around real placed neighbours) — is the visible imprint of curvature on dodecahedral symmetry.

"The geometry is not decorative. A dodecahedron has 12 pentagonal faces, 20 vertices, 30 edges. 20 vertices = 20 named structural elements of the theory. Click them."

The twelve mysteries

The questions come from the SpookyPrimes research programme — a machine-verified computational study of the algebraic structure of the Standard Model in Connes' noncommutative geometry framework. Each pentagon is an unresolved question that programme either answered, failed to answer, or precisely located.

  • Why this algebra and not another? (Step 4 closure)
  • Three different inputs, identical output (the 16-dimensional plateau)
  • Why does it take two conditions?
  • Why are quarks and leptons different? (leptoquark carveout)
  • Why exactly three generations?
  • A suspiciously precise coincidence (Koide K ≈ 2/3)
  • The worst prediction in physics (cosmological constant, 10¹²⁰)
  • Why does any of this work at all? (Wigner)
  • Why these specific signs? (KO-dimension 6)
  • Does the spectral action actually do its job?
  • Why these particular masses? (Yukawa hierarchy)
  • Why do flavors mix the way they do? (CKM, PMNS)

Click any envelope face in the live visualization to read the question and its implicated structural elements.

How it's built

One HTML file, no build step, no bundler. Three.js loaded as an ESM module from cdnjs. ~2700 lines of vanilla JS and WebGL.

The Möbius is parameterized as (u, v) → vertexPos(u, v) over u ∈ [0, 2π), v ∈ [-WIDTH, WIDTH], with the central radius R = 3.0 and width 1.25. Surface normals are computed numerically and forced outward for consistent shading.

The dodecahedron envelope is derived at load time from φ = (1 + √5)/2. Twenty vertices: 8 cube corners plus 12 from the golden ratio construction. Thirty edges: vertex pairs at minimum mutual distance. Twelve faces: coplanar 5-cycles in the adjacency graph. The math is identical to the math in the SpookyPrimes companion repo — re-implemented in this codebase's style.

A unified scale module ties pentagon size to lattice density. Change U_STEPS and every pentagon-related size (placed, ghost neighbours, fold-up/down, amber flower) rescales proportionally — same code path, single source of truth.

The doctrine

Three things this codebase commits to. They're written here so anyone reading the source can see what the architecture is for.

1 · Markdown is truth

All canonical state lives in .md files that humans can read. The visualization is a window into that truth, not a replacement for it. When the visualization disagrees with the markdown, the markdown wins.

2 · AI as guest, not admin

Any AI working in this codebase proposes changes; humans commit them. The codebase's structure (§A–§O section markers, single-source-of-truth scale module, named-constant palette) makes that easy. An agent can read 50 lines and know where to put a change.

3 · Cave-dweller exit clause

Every feature can be turned off. The void-mode slider is one expression of this — push it to max and the visualization gracefully erases itself, leaving only the chrome the user controls. Nothing breaks, nothing errors. Pull back, everything returns. The user is always in charge of how much of the experience is on.
