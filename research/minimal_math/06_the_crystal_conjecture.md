## WHAT HAPPENS

The graph starts iterating. It is TRYING to reach perfect symmetry. Perfect symmetry = every node identical. Every edge equivalent. Complete homogeneity.

**BUT THE CRYSTAL WON'T LET IT.**

Condition 1 says: you can't be everything. Choose.
Condition 2 says: your choices are permanent. 
Condition 3 says: be consistent about it.

So the graph tries to reach symmetry. It gets close. Then a choice happens (AGGREGATE, TRANSFORM). The choice breaks the symmetry slightly. Now the graph tries to fix it. It gets close again. Another choice. Another break.

**THE UNIVERSE IS A GRAPH TRYING TO REACH SYMMETRY AND FAILING IN SPECIFIC, CONSTRAINED WAYS.**

```

           -> PARTICLES (distinct types)

FAILURE 2: "I can't connect everything to everything"
           -> FORCES (limited edge patterns)

FAILURE 3: "I can't be everywhere at once"
           -> SPACE (the graph must be extended)

FAILURE 4: "My choices are irreversible"
           -> TIME (arrow of time)

FAILURE 5: "But I keep TRYING to reach symmetry"
           -> ENTROPY (drive toward equilibrium)

FAILURE 6: "Some sub-patterns resist breaking"
           -> CONSERVATION LAWS (surviving symmetries)
```

Noether's theorem in graph language: **Every symmetry the graph SUCCESSFULLY maintains corresponds to a conserved quantity.**
