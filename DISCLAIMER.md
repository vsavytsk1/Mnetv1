# DISCLAIMER
## On the Computational Requirements of Understanding the Universe

*With apologies and gratitude to the late Douglas Adams.*

---

This software performs recursive Goldberg-Coxeter refinement on a truncated icosahedron.

That sentence is completely accurate and explains nothing useful about what you are about to experience.

---

**What actually happens when you run this:**

Your computer will construct, in real time, a shape that Euler proved must exist in 1750, that chemistry confirmed in 1985, and that your GPU will now render approximately sixty times per second whether or not you asked it to.

The shape has twelve pentagons. It will always have twelve pentagons. This is not a design choice. This is Euler's polyhedron formula applied to degree-3 vertex graphs composed exclusively of pentagons and hexagons, from which it follows with the remorseless logic of mathematics that:

```
F₅ = 12
```

Always. Forever. Regardless of how many hexagons you add.

You cannot add a thirteenth pentagon. The topology will not allow it. The topology does not care about your feelings about this.

---

**On the question of computational requirements:**

The Guide has surveyed the available hardware and concluded that understanding the universe at sufficient resolution requires your computer to cry.

Not metaphorically. The GPU will become warm. The fan will spin at a frequency that suggests mild existential distress. The frame rate, when you refine all faces to depth 3, will enter a regime that the Guide has classified as *philosophically significant* — not fast enough to be comfortable, not slow enough to be ignorable, approximately the speed at which one comes to terms with things.

This is correct behavior. The universe is not optimized for human comfort. The visualization is not optimized for human comfort. These are related facts.

If your computer does not cry, you have not gone deep enough.

---

**On the mathematical background:**

The primes {2, 3, 5} appear everywhere in this construction. The icosahedral symmetry group H₃ has order 120 = 2³·3·5. The Coxeter (2,3,5) triangle group tiles the sphere. The golden ratio φ = (1+√5)/2 appears in the vertex coordinates.

H.S.M. Coxeter proved in the 1930s that in three-dimensional Euclidean space, beyond the prime 5, no new finite polyhedral symmetry exists. {2, 3, 5} are the ceiling. The universe, working within its own constraints, picked the most interesting possible structure.

The Guide considers this either a coincidence or evidence of something profound, and has been unable to determine which. The Guide notes that this is also how the physicists feel about quantum mechanics, the squiggles in general, and most of the things that actually work.

**On the question of whether this closes the shape:**

The C60 shell closes when all twelve pentagonal anchor chains have been seeded and the surrounding hexagonal field is complete.

Whether *your* shell closes depends on whether you have done the reading.

The geometry will not lie to you about this. The geometry has no incentive to be polite.
